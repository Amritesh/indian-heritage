Scholarly Commentary

"The Silk Road was never a single path, but a network of trade, dialogue, and spiritual exchange. For the Digital Curator, these artifacts are more than objects; they are witnesses to the first era of globalization in the Indian subcontinent."

Dr. Amitav Varma

Senior Curator, Indian Numismatics Dept.

Research suggests that the Kushan Empire acted as the primary bridge between the Han Dynasty and the Roman Empire. Our challenge focuses on the transition from Hellenistic aesthetic ideals to the formalized iconography of early Buddhist art, a transformation best seen in the Gandhara region.
