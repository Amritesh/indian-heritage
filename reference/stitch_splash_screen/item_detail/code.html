Historical Significance

This "Archer Type" Dinara represents the zenith of the Gupta Empire's numismatic art. Struck during the reign of Samudragupta, often termed the 'Napoleon of India', the coin depicts the king standing left, holding a bow in his left hand and an arrow in his right.

The reverse features Goddess Lakshmi seated on a lotus, holding a cornucopia and a diadem. This iconography signifies the prosperity and divine sanction of the Gupta rule. The Brahmi legend "Apratirathah" (The Matchless Warrior) is inscribed around the king, highlighting his military prowess.
